# 🛍️ Customer Shopping Behavior Analysis

End-to-end data analytics project that explores consumer shopping behavior using **Python (data cleaning & EDA)**, **SQL (business analysis)**, and **Power BI (interactive dashboard)** to help a retail company understand what drives customer engagement, spending, and loyalty.

---

## 📌 Business Problem

A leading retail company wants to better understand its customers' shopping behavior in order to improve sales, customer satisfaction, and long-term loyalty. This project analyzes the company's consumer shopping dataset to answer:

> **"How can the company leverage consumer shopping data to identify trends, improve customer engagement, and optimize marketing and product strategies?"**

## 🧹 Data Preparation (Python)

- Loaded raw dataset (3,900 rows × 18 columns) using **pandas**
- Checked structure (`df.info()`) and summary statistics (`df.describe()`)
- Handled 37 missing values in `Review Rating` by imputing the **median rating per product category**
- Standardized column names to **snake_case**
- Engineered new features:
  - `age_group` (binned customer ages: Young Adult, Adult, Middle-aged, Senior)
  - `purchase_frequency_days`
- Checked redundancy between `discount_applied` and `promo_code_used`, dropped the latter
- Loaded the cleaned dataset into **PostgreSQL** for SQL analysis

## 💡 Key Insights

- **Male customers generated significantly more total revenue** (~$157.9K) than female customers (~$75.2K).
- **839 customers** used a discount yet still spent above the average purchase amount — discounts aren't purely price-driven behavior.
- Top-rated products: **Gloves, Sandals, Boots, Hat, Skirt** (avg. rating ~3.8+).
- **Express shipping** customers spend slightly more on average ($60.48) than **Standard shipping** customers ($58.46).
- **Non-subscribers generate far higher total revenue** ($170K) than subscribers ($62.6K) — despite subscribers being fewer, individual subscriber spend is comparable, so growing the subscriber base has upside.
- **Loyal customers dominate** the base (3,116 of 3,900), followed by Returning (701) and New (83).
- **Hat, Sneakers, Coat, Sweater, Pants** are the most discount-dependent products.
- **Young Adults** are the highest revenue-contributing age group, closely followed by Middle-aged, Adult, and Senior segments.

---

## ✅ Business Recommendations

- **Boost Subscriptions** — Promote exclusive benefits for subscribers to grow this high-potential segment.
- **Customer Loyalty Programs** — Reward repeat buyers to convert Returning customers into Loyal ones.
- **Review Discount Policy** — Balance sales boosts from discounts with margin control on discount-dependent products.
- **Product Positioning** — Highlight top-rated and best-selling products in marketing campaigns.
- **Targeted Marketing** — Focus efforts on high-revenue age groups and express-shipping users.
